1. Executive Summary

Spectre is a security weakness found in many modern computer processors. These processors try to work faster by “guessing” what they will need to do next. Normally this guessing is harmless, but Spectre shows that it can be tricked into revealing information that should stay private.

In our demonstration, we show how an attacker can influence a processor’s guesses and then observe small clues left behind to figure out a piece of hidden data—such as part of a password. This does not break the software itself; instead, it takes advantage of the way the hardware is built.

The goal of our project is to explain how this type of vulnerability works in an easy-to-understand way and to show a simple example of how private information can be leaked when processors behave unexpectedly.
